    • A Snapshot of The Social Feed.

    The Social Feed was a communal dining concept, that doubled as a restaurant guide. Followers would browse and reserve communal meals online, then attend the events to meet other like-minded individuals while experiencing a restaurant tasting menu.
    The service aimed at taking social networks offline, while providing advertising opportunities for those involved.
    In July of 2012, The Social Feed was acquired by Grubwithus.

    What follows is a snapshot of the work produced for The Social Feed.

    Digital Design

    The Social Feed was web based, so most of the content produced lived online. We tried to create a brand identity that felt like a virtual restaurant. A brand that existed online, but also in the restaurants we partnered with.

    Print Design.

    Restaurant Writeups

    The Electric Owl
    In case you were wondering, The Social Feed is huge in Japan. Another greatness huge in Japan - {Izakaya's} - causal places for after work eating and drinking, with emphasis on the latter. And because all great inventions come from Japan {proof}, the Electric Owl took a little inspiration from the Izakaya concept and ran with it, all the way to Main street {and Georgia}. Housed in the old American Hotel building and opened last May, why not drop by and "eat Japanese style snacks, prepared by a Vietnamese chef, in an Izakaya-inspired room, followed by a fortune cookie and a live band?" Gyozas, Nori Pockets, and Dipping Noodles at 7:25, followed by a DJ at 9 and a reggae band at 10. Good food and good people followed by good music. Don't forget to bring your {noodle face shield.}

    Cork & Fin
    It's getting harder to name a restaurant. All the good names are {taken}. Consequently, it's probably best to leave the puns at home and cut right to the chase. Cork & Fin have done just that, choosing to showcase what they do best, market fresh seafood and a great selection of whites and reds. Finding a slice of historic Gastown, (just off the entrance to the infamous Blood Alley) in the spring of 2010 owners Francis Regio and Elliot Hashimoto transformed one of Vancouver's oldest spaces into casual dining with a raw bar for everyone's favourite bi-valve. Featuring locally sourced seafood such as sawmill bay oysters, this Feed brings together great people and great food. Social-ly Feed-nominal, perhaps?

    Habit Lounge
    If you're under the impression that Canada's national treasures consist of coins {two-big} and {Bieber-mania}, this Feed awaits to pleasantly surprise you and introduce the real Canadian goods - Canadian whisky. And there's no better place to introduce such {Canadian Delights} than along a family style meal served up fresh at {Habit Lounge}. With decor designed from the concept of 70's Canadiana, Habit sets out to create a gathering place where the local neighbourhood comes to meet and you're assured of feeling right at home. For this Feed, Habit Lounge serves up the comfort food - Blood Alley Bitter Braised Beef and homemade pie. As usual, no specific dress code (causal/trendy, leave the sweatpants at home), but if you choose to show up in Canada's lesser known treasure, the {Canadian Tuxedo}, the whisky might just be on the house.

    Peckinpah BBQ
    If you live in Van, there are two ways to experience Carolina BBQ. One involves inviting an accident by crushing 49 hours of interstate highways to get to {Raleigh}, or if you don't feel like riskin' it for that brisket, jump on your fixie and cruise down to Peckingpah BBQ in Gastown. Peckinpah stays true to North Carolina BBQ using a cookhouse smoker fuled by Tennessee white oak and serving the sauce proper - vinegar base with a hint of chilli. This pork-centric menu showcases all the heavy hitters including pork ribs, pulled pork, and smoked brisket accompanied by all the fixins. The only thing more impressive then this marathon of meat is Peckinpah's list of nearly 20 bourbons on the back shelf. Seriously, please don't go drive to Carolina. There's no point in leaving for the ball diamond if you've got a perfectly good patch of lawn in the backyard.
